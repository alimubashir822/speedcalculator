Double Discount Calculator — Why Stacked Discounts Are Less Than You Think

The most important insight from a double discount calculator: two discounts applied sequentially are never equal to their sum. A 20% discount followed by a 15% discount is NOT 35% off — it is only 32% effective. This is because the second discount applies to the already-reduced price, not the original. Retailers use this to make deals sound better than they are.

Double Discount Calculator Example

$100 item · First discount: 20% · Second discount: 15%
Step 1: $100 × (1 − 0.20) = $100 × 0.80 = $80
Step 2: $80 × (1 − 0.15) = $80 × 0.85 = $68
Total saved: $32 · Effective discount: 32% (NOT 35%)
The difference: if it were 35% off, final price = $65 (you'd save $3 more)

Triple Discount Calculator — Stacking Three Discounts

The triple discount calculator extends the same sequential logic to three discounts. Effective Triple Discount = 1 − (1−D1) × (1−D2) × (1−D3). A 20% + 10% + 5% triple discount has an effective rate of only 31.6%, not 35%.

Double discount calculator: $200, 20%+15% → $136 final (effective 32%, not 35%). Triple discount calculator: $200, 20%+10%+5% → $136.80 (effective 31.6%, not 35%)

Discount Calculator with Tax — Sales Tax and Discount

Our discount calculator with tax and sales tax and discount calculator follow the correct order of operations: tax is always applied to the post-discount price, not the original price. The formula: Final = Original × (1 − Discount%) × (1 + Tax%).

Tax and Discount Calculator Example

$200 TV · 25% off · 8.5% sales tax
After discount: $200 × 0.75 = $150
With tax: $150 × 1.085 = $162.75
Total paid vs. full price with tax: $200 × 1.085 = $217 vs $162.75
Total savings including tax: $54.25

Discount Calculator FAQ

Questions answered for percent discount calculator, double discount, triple discount, discount with tax, bulk discount, GM employee discount, Best Buy employee discount, Progressive Snapshot discount, and more.

How do I calculate percent off a price?

Formula: Final Price = Original Price × (1 − Discount% ÷ 100). Amount Saved = Original Price × (Discount% ÷ 100). Quick mental math: for 20% off any price, multiply by 0.80. For 30% off, multiply by 0.70. For 25% off, multiply by 0.75. Our percent discount calculator handles any percentage instantly — enter the original price, enter the discount %, and click Calculate. The Quick Presets (10%, 15%, 20%, 25%, 30%, 40%, 50%) let you calculate common discounts in one click without typing.

How does the double discount calculator work?

A double discount applies two successive discounts where the second applies to the already-reduced price. Formula: Final = Price × (1−D1/100) × (1−D2/100). Example: $100 item, 20% off then 15% off: $100 × 0.80 = $80, then $80 × 0.85 = $68. Effective discount = 32%, NOT 35% (which would give $65). Our double discount calculator shows the step-by-step breakdown and the true effective discount percentage, so you can compare stacked discounts accurately.

How does a discount calculator with tax work?

Our discount calculator with tax applies tax to the post-discount price (which is how stores legally calculate it). Formula: Final = (Price × (1−Discount%)) × (1+Tax%). Example: $150 item, 30% off, 8% tax: After discount: $150 × 0.70 = $105. With tax: $105 × 1.08 = $113.40. If you had paid full price with tax: $150 × 1.08 = $162. Total savings including tax = $162 − $113.40 = $48.60. Use the "With Tax" mode in our sales tax and discount calculator for complete pricing.

What is a 10 discount calculator — how much is 10% off any price?

A 10% discount calculator is simple — just multiply the price by 0.90 (or divide by 10 to find the discount amount). 10% off $50 = $45 (save $5). 10% off $200 = $180 (save $20). 10% off $89.99 = $80.99 (save $9). Quick mental trick: 10% off any price = move the decimal point one place left. $150 → 10% = $15 savings → final price $135. Click the "10% Off" preset button in our calculator above for instant results at any price.

How do I find the original price from a discounted price?

Formula: Original Price = Sale Price ÷ (1 − Discount% ÷ 100). Example: item now costs $68, it's 15% off → Original = $68 ÷ (1 − 0.15) = $68 ÷ 0.85 = $80. Use "Find Original Price" mode in our price discount calculator: enter the discounted price and the discount percentage to instantly find what the item cost before the markdown. Useful for checking if a "sale" price is really a discount, or for verifying retailer pricing claims.

How does a triple discount calculator differ from adding percentages?

Adding three discount percentages together is always wrong for stacked discounts. Our triple discount calculator shows the correct sequential calculation: 20% + 10% + 5% stacked on $100: Step 1: $100 × 0.80 = $80. Step 2: $80 × 0.90 = $72. Step 3: $72 × 0.95 = $68.40. Effective discount = 31.6%, NOT 35%. The formula: Effective = 1 − (0.80 × 0.90 × 0.95) = 1 − 0.684 = 31.6%. Always use our triple discount calculator instead of manually adding percentages for accurate results.

What is the bulk discount calculator used for?

The bulk discount calculator is for calculating savings when buying multiple units at a discount. It shows: Unit price after discount, Total cost for quantity, Total savings vs full price, and Cost per unit. Example: Buy 50 units at $12 each with 30% bulk discount: Discounted unit price = $8.40. Total cost = $420. Savings vs full price ($600) = $180. Perfect for wholesale buyers, resellers, office supply purchases, or any quantity purchasing decision. Select "Bulk" mode in our retail discount calculator above.
