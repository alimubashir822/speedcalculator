Calorie Calculator for Weight Loss — How Many Calories to Lose Weight?

Our calorie calculator for weight loss and weight loss calorie calculator use a 500 calorie/day deficit below TDEE, which produces approximately 1 pound (0.45 kg) of fat loss per week (based on 3,500 calories = 1 lb fat). For faster loss, use a 750 cal/day deficit (~1.5 lbs/week). Never go below 1,200 calories (women) or 1,500 calories (men) without medical supervision — extremely low calories cause muscle loss, metabolic adaptation, and nutrient deficiencies.

Calorie Calculator to Lose Weight — Example

Woman, 35, 165cm, 75kg, Moderately Active
BMR = 10×75 + 6.25×165 − 5×35 − 161 = 1,514 cal
TDEE = 1,514 × 1.55 = 2,347 cal/day (maintenance)
Weight loss target (−500): 1,847 cal/day → ~1 lb/week loss
Faster loss (−750): 1,597 cal/day → ~1.5 lbs/week loss

Maintenance Calorie Calculator — What Is TDEE?

The maintenance calorie calculator computes your TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure) — the exact number of calories you need each day to maintain your current weight. TDEE = BMR × Activity Factor. At your TDEE, body weight stays constant. Eat above = gain weight. Eat below = lose weight. Most adults have a TDEE between 1,800–3,000 calories depending on size and activity level. Our online calorie calculator computes your exact TDEE using the Mifflin-St Jeor formula.

Dog Calorie Calculator & Cat Calorie Calculator

Looking for a dog calorie calculator or cat calorie calculator? While our tool focuses on humans, here are general pet guidelines: Dogs need approximately 30 × body weight (kg) + 70 calories per day for maintenance (the RER formula). Cats need approximately 70 × body weight (kg)^0.75 calories per day. For example, a 10kg (22 lb) dog needs about 370 calories/day, while a 4.5kg (10 lb) cat needs about 200 calories/day. Always consult your veterinarian for precise pet nutrition guidance, especially for weight loss, growing pets, or those with health conditions.

Common Questions

Calorie Calculator FAQ

Questions covering daily calorie needs, weight loss, TDEE, BMR, running/walking calorie burn, and food calorie lookups.

How many calories should I eat per day?

Daily calorie needs vary significantly by age, gender, size, and activity level. General ranges: Women 1,600–2,400 cal/day; Men 2,000–3,000 cal/day. For your exact number, use our daily calorie calculator above — it uses the Mifflin-St Jeor formula, the most accurate available. Your TDEE (maintenance calories) is the foundation: eat less to lose weight, eat more to gain. For weight loss: TDEE − 500 = ~1 lb/week loss. For weight gain: TDEE + 500 = ~1 lb/week gain. Never go below 1,200 (women) or 1,500 (men) calories without medical supervision.

How does the maintenance calorie calculator work?

Our maintenance calorie calculator computes TDEE (Total Daily Energy Expenditure) in two steps: 1) Calculate BMR (Basal Metabolic Rate) using the Mifflin-St Jeor formula. 2) Multiply BMR by your activity factor: Sedentary ×1.2, Lightly Active ×1.375, Moderately Active ×1.55, Very Active ×1.725, Extra Active ×1.9. TDEE = your maintenance calories. Eating exactly at TDEE = stable weight. This is the most important number from our online calorie calculator.

How many calories does running burn?

Our running calorie calculator uses MET (Metabolic Equivalent) values. Formula: Calories = MET × weight(kg) × hours. Running at 6 mph: MET = 10. A 70kg person running 30 minutes = 10 × 70 × 0.5 = 350 calories. Running at 8 mph: MET = 13.5 = 473 cal/30min. Our treadmill calorie calculator is nearly identical — treadmill burns slightly less due to no wind resistance. Use the Exercise Burn tab above to calculate your specific burn based on your weight, duration, and speed.

How many calories in a Chipotle bowl?

Our Chipotle calorie calculator / Chipotle bowl calorie calculator breakdown: Chicken (4oz) = 190 cal · White Rice = 210 cal · Black Beans = 130 cal · Cheese = 110 cal · Sour Cream = 120 cal · Guacamole = 230 cal · Salsa (fresh) = 20 cal. A fully-loaded chicken bowl = ~1,010–1,200 cal. Lower calorie bowl: Chicken + Lettuce base + Black beans + Fajita veggies + Fresh salsa = ~500–600 cal. Use our Food tab for more details. Switch rice for lettuce (save ~200 cal) and skip cheese + sour cream (save ~230 cal).

What is the Mifflin-St Jeor formula and why use it?

The Mifflin-St Jeor formula is the most validated BMR equation for most adults. It was derived in 1990 and is the formula recommended by the Academy of Nutrition and Dietetics. Men: BMR = 10×weight(kg) + 6.25×height(cm) − 5×age + 5. Women: BMR = 10×weight(kg) + 6.25×height(cm) − 5×age − 161. It's ~5% more accurate than the older Harris-Benedict formula for predicting resting metabolic rate in most adults. Our daily calorie calculator uses Mifflin-St Jeor exclusively, which is why it's more accurate than many competitors' tools that still use Harris-Benedict.

How many calories should I eat to gain weight?

Our calorie calculator to gain weight recommends eating 250–500 calories above your TDEE. A 250 cal/day surplus = ~0.5 lb/week gain (better for lean gains). A 500 cal/day surplus = ~1 lb/week gain. Combine with strength training to ensure you're building muscle, not just fat. Aim for 0.8–1g protein per lb of bodyweight during a bulk. If you're gaining more than 1 lb/week, reduce the surplus — excess calories above muscle synthesis capacity = fat gain.
