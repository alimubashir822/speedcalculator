Net Salary Calculator — How Take-Home Pay Is Calculated

Our net salary calculator computes take-home pay by subtracting all mandatory deductions from gross pay:

  • Federal Income Tax: Progressive rates from 10% to 37% based on taxable income and filing status (2024 brackets)
  • Social Security: 6.2% on wages up to $168,600 (2024 wage base)
  • Medicare: 1.45% on all wages (additional 0.9% on wages over $200K single)
  • State Income Tax: 0% (Texas, Florida, Nevada) to 13.3% (California top bracket)
  • Pre-Tax Deductions: 401(k), health insurance, FSA — reduce your taxable income

Common Questions

Salary Calculator FAQ

Answers for hourly to salary calculator, take home salary calculator, annual salary calculator, monthly salary calculator, and state-specific questions.

How do I convert hourly to annual salary?

The hourly to annual salary calculator formula is: Annual = Hourly Rate × Hours Per Week × Weeks Per Year. For standard full-time work: Annual = Hourly × 2,080 (40 hours × 52 weeks). Quick reference: $15/hr = $31,200/yr · $20/hr = $41,600/yr · $25/hr = $52,000/yr · $30/hr = $62,400/yr · $50/hr = $104,000/yr. Our hourly to salary calculator handles any hours/weeks combination, including overtime at 1.5x for hours over 40/week. Switch to "Hourly Wage" mode, enter your rate, and see the complete annual, monthly, bi-weekly breakdown instantly.

How much is $60,000 a year per hour?

$60,000 ÷ 2,080 hours (40hrs × 52wks) = $28.85/hour. Monthly gross = $5,000. Bi-weekly gross = $2,307.69. Weekly gross = $1,153.85. After taxes (single filer, Texas/no state tax): estimated take-home ≈ $47,650/year ($3,971/month). Use our annual salary calculator — switch to "Annual Salary" mode, enter $60,000, and all conversions appear instantly including take-home by state.

What is the difference between gross salary and net take-home pay?

Gross salary is your total compensation before any deductions — the number in your offer letter. Net salary (take-home pay) is what actually hits your bank account after: federal income tax (10–37%), state income tax (0–13.3%), Social Security (6.2%), Medicare (1.45%), and any pre-tax deductions (401k, health insurance). For most middle-income earners, take-home is 70–80% of gross in no-state-tax states, or 65–75% in high-tax states like California and NYC. Our net salary calculator shows this full breakdown for every pay period.

How does the take-home salary calculator work for California vs Texas?

Texas and Florida have no state income tax, so our salary calculator Texas and Florida salary calculator show the highest take-home for any given gross salary. The California salary calculator uses CA's progressive brackets (1%–13.3%) — for a $80,000 salary in California, effective state tax is approximately 6–7%, costing about $4,800–5,600/year more than Texas. Select your state in the calculator above and see the instant difference. This is why the salary calculator california results are often surprisingly lower than people expect.

How do I use the NYC salary calculator and salary calculator New York?

NYC residents pay three income taxes: federal + NY State (up to 10.9%) + NYC city tax (up to 3.876%). Select "New York City" in the state dropdown of our NYC salary calculator to include all three. For NY state residents outside NYC, select "New York" — this includes only federal + state tax. Our salary calculator New York estimates show that a $100K NYC salary nets approximately $61,950/year take-home — about $10,400 less than the same salary in Texas, entirely due to state and city taxes.

What is bi-weekly pay and how is it calculated?

Bi-weekly pay means receiving a paycheck every two weeks — 26 paychecks per year. Gross bi-weekly = Annual Salary ÷ 26. For $60,000/year: $60,000 ÷ 26 = $2,307.69 per paycheck (gross). Note: two months of the year have three bi-weekly paychecks instead of two — useful for budget planning. This is the most common US pay schedule, used by most employers including ADP payroll systems. Our monthly salary calculator and bi-weekly calculator show both gross and net for every period.

How do 401(k) contributions affect take-home pay?

Traditional 401(k) contributions are pre-tax — they reduce your taxable income. If you contribute $5,000/year and you're in the 22% federal bracket, you avoid $1,100 in federal tax (plus state tax savings). The "real cost" of a $5,000 contribution is only about $3,400–3,600 after tax savings. The 2024 limit is $23,000 ($30,500 if 50+). Use the Pre-Tax Deductions toggle in our take home salary calculator to enter your 401(k) amount and see the exact impact on your monthly and annual take-home pay. This is the most powerful legal tool to reduce taxes while building retirement savings.
