Creatine Water Intake Calculator — Why You Need More Water

The creatine water intake calculator and water intake calculator with creatine add extra hydration because creatine has a unique osmotic effect — it draws water from your bloodstream into your muscle cells (intramyocellular water). This reduces available circulatory water, making increased intake essential. Standard recommendation: add 500ml for maintenance doses (3–5g/day). During the loading phase (20g/day for 5–7 days), many sports dietitians recommend adding 1,000–1,500ml extra daily. Signs of creatine-related dehydration include muscle cramps, headaches, and reduced exercise performance.

NHS Water Intake Calculator — UK Recommendations

The NHS water intake calculator approach recommends 6–8 glasses of fluid per day (1.5–2 liters) as a baseline for adults in the UK's temperate climate. This is a minimum, not an optimal target. The NHS recommendation is lower than the US (3.7L men, 2.7L women) because the UK climate is cooler and less sweat-inducing. Common Questions

Water Intake Calculator FAQ

Answers for daily water needs, creatine hydration, keto water intake, dog and cat water needs, weight loss hydration, and the NHS water recommendation.

How much water should I drink per day?

Daily water needs depend on weight, age, activity, and climate. The most practical formula for the recommended water intake calculator: Weight (kg) × 35ml = daily baseline. A 70kg moderately active adult in a temperate climate: 70 × 35 = 2,450ml base + 500ml activity = 2,950ml (2.95 liters/day). The NHS recommends a minimum of 6–8 glasses (1.5–2L) for UK adults, while the US recommends 3.7L for men and 2.7L for women (including water from food). Use our daily water intake calculator for your exact personalized amount.

How much extra water do I need with creatine?

Our creatine water intake calculator adds 500ml above baseline for standard creatine use (3–5g/day maintenance). During the loading phase (20g/day for 5–7 days), some experts recommend 1,000–1,500ml extra. The reason: creatine is stored in muscles with water (1g creatine binds ~3–4g water). This pulls water from blood into muscles, reducing circulatory hydration. Signs you're not drinking enough with creatine: muscle cramps, headaches, bloating, darker urine. Enable "Creatine" in our water intake calculator with creatine toggle for your adjusted daily target.

How much water should I drink on a keto diet?

Our keto water intake calculator adds 500ml above your baseline. Keto dehydration happens for two reasons: 1) Glycogen depletion (reducing carbs from 300g to near-zero) releases ~600–800ml of water from muscle glycogen stores. 2) Low insulin levels cause kidneys to excrete more sodium and water. Electrolytes are also lost — sodium, potassium, and magnesium. On keto, drink an extra 500ml–1L of water daily and add electrolytes. Enable "Keto Diet" in our calculator for your keto-adjusted water intake target.

How much water does my dog or cat need per day?

Our dog water intake calculator: Dogs need approximately 55ml of water per kg of body weight per day. A 15kg Labrador needs ~825ml (0.8L) daily. Dogs on dry kibble need 20–30% more. Puppies and nursing dogs need 1.5× more. Our cat water intake calculator: Cats need approximately 60ml per kg per day. A 4.5kg cat needs ~270ml daily. Cats are notoriously poor drinkers — they evolved as desert animals. Cats on dry food are at high risk of UTIs and kidney disease from chronic mild dehydration. Switch to wet food or use a cat water fountain. Select "Dog" or "Cat" mode in our calculator above for exact amounts.

Does drinking more water help with weight loss?

Yes — research supports water for weight loss in multiple ways: 1) Drinking 500ml before meals reduces calorie intake by ~13% (2015 RCT, University of Birmingham). 2) Cold water boosts metabolic rate by 24–30% for 30–40 minutes post-drink. 3) Water replaces calorie-containing drinks (reducing 1 soda/day = ~150 cal less). 4) Adequate hydration improves fat oxidation (lipolysis). Our water intake calculator for weight loss / water intake calculator to lose weight adds 300ml to baseline — targeting 2.5–3.5+ liters daily depending on weight. Enable the "Weight Loss" goal in the calculator.

What does the NHS recommend for daily water intake?

The NHS water intake calculator recommendation: 6–8 glasses of fluid per day (approximately 1.5–2 liters) for adults in the UK. This includes water, lower-fat milk, and sugar-free drinks — but not alcohol. The NHS notes that tea and coffee count but caffeinated drinks have a mild diuretic effect. The NHS recommendation is a minimum baseline for temperate climates and sedentary lifestyles. Our ideal water intake calculator aligns with NHS guidance at the "Sedentary, Temperate" setting and scales up for activity, climate, and special conditions. For most active adults in warmer climates, 2.5–3L is a more appropriate target.
