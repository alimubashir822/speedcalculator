What Is RPM and Why Does It Matter for Speed?

RPM stands for Revolutions Per Minute — how many complete rotations your engine crankshaft makes every minute. Your tachometer displays this number. At idle, most car engines run at 700–900 RPM. On a highway at 60 mph, you might be at 2,000–2,500 RPM in top gear.

RPM alone doesn't tell you vehicle speed. You also need to know how the engine's rotation translates to wheel rotation — and that depends on your transmission gear ratio, rear axle ratio, and tire diameter. Together, these four values give you an exact vehicle speed.

💡
Quick Fact: Two cars at the same RPM can be traveling at very different speeds if they have different tire sizes or gear ratios. A lifted truck with 35-inch tires travels further per revolution than a sedan with 25-inch tires — even at the same RPM and gear.

The RPM to Speed Formula

The core formula for converting RPM to speed in mph:

RPM to MPH Formula
Speed (mph) = (RPM × π × Tire Diameter) ÷ (Gear Ratio × Axle Ratio × 1056)
Where: Tire Diameter is in inches · The constant 1056 = (12 in/ft × 5280 ft/mile) ÷ 60 sec/min
RPM to KM/H Formula
Speed (km/h) = (RPM × π × Tire Diameter in mm × 60) ÷ (Gear Ratio × Axle Ratio × 1,000,000)
Or simply: Speed (km/h) = Speed (mph) × 1.60934

What Each Variable Means

  • RPM — Your tachometer reading. Use the current engine RPM you want to calculate for.
  • π (Pi) — 3.14159. Converts diameter to circumference (distance per rotation).
  • Tire Diameter — The overall outer diameter of your tire in inches. Not the rim size — the whole tire including sidewalls.
  • Gear Ratio — Your transmission gear ratio for the current gear (e.g., 4th gear = 1.00:1, 1st gear = 3.50:1).
  • Axle Ratio — Your rear differential (axle) ratio (e.g., 3.73:1). Printed on a sticker in your door jam or in your owner's manual.

How to Find Your Tire Diameter from the Tire Size Code

Your tire sidewall shows a code like 225/45R17. Here's what each number means and how to calculate diameter:

Tire Diameter Formula from Size Code
Diameter = (2 × Width × Aspect/100) + (Rim × 25.4) — all in mm, then ÷ 25.4 for inches
For 225/45R17: Sidewall = 225 × 0.45 = 101.25mm · Diameter = (2 × 101.25) + (17 × 25.4) = 634.3mm = 24.97"

Understanding Gear Ratios and Axle Ratios

The gear ratio and axle ratio work together to multiply the engine RPM reduction down to wheel RPM. A total drive ratio of 3.73:1 means your engine turns 3.73 times for every one wheel revolution.

⚠
Important: If you change your tire size (e.g., upgrade to 35-inch off-road tires from 31-inch stock tires), your speedometer will read incorrectly. Larger tires = higher actual speed than displayed. Use our calculator to find the exact speedometer error percentage.

RPM to Speed for Bicycles and Motorcycles

For bicycles, the calculation is simpler because pedal cadence (RPM) relates directly to wheel speed through the chainring/sprocket ratio. No axle ratio needed:

Bicycle Speed from Cadence (RPM)
Wheel RPM = Cadence × (Chainring Teeth ÷ Sprocket Teeth)
Speed (km/h) = Wheel RPM × π × Wheel Diameter (m) × 60 ÷ 1000
Example: 90 RPM cadence, 50T/15T gear, 700c wheel (0.622m) → 35.2 km/h
