Cylinder Volume Calculator

The cylinder volume calculator uses the formula V = pi x r2 x h. This is the most commonly used volume formula because cylinders appear everywhere — water tanks, pipes, cans, wells, columns, and pools. If you know the diameter instead of the radius, divide by 2 first.

Example — Water storage tank

Tank with diameter 6 ft and height 8 ft: r = 3 ft, V = pi x 9 x 8 = 226.19 ft3 = 1,691.6 gallons = 6,404 litres

Cone Volume Calculator

The cone volume calculator uses V = (1/3) x pi x r2 x h. A cone always holds exactly one-third the volume of a cylinder with the same base radius and height. Use this for grain silos, hoppers, traffic cones, and ice cream cone volumes.

Sphere Volume Calculator

The sphere volume calculator uses V = (4/3) x pi x r3. Volume increases with the cube of the radius — so doubling the radius increases volume by 8 times. Common applications include balls, globes, spherical tanks, and gas storage spheres.

Triangular Prism Volume Calculator

A triangular prism volume calculator uses V = (1/2) x base x height x length. Enter the base and height of the triangular cross-section, then the length (depth) of the prism. Used for roof volumes, Toblerone-shaped packaging, and drainage channels with triangular cross-sections.

Pool Volume Calculator

To use our pool volume calculator, identify your pool shape first. A rectangular pool uses the box formula: V = L x W x D. A round (circular) pool uses the cylinder formula: V = pi x r2 x depth. All results automatically convert to US gallons so you can calculate chlorine and chemical dosages correctly.

Example — Rectangular swimming pool

Pool 30 ft x 15 ft x 5 ft deep: V = 30 x 15 x 5 = 2,250 ft3 = 16,832 US gallons = 63,706 litres

Tank Volume Calculator

Our tank volume calculator handles both cylindrical tanks (vertical and horizontal) and rectangular tanks. For a horizontal cylindrical tank, the formula depends on the fill level, which our calculator handles automatically. Tank volumes are used for fuel oil, water storage, chemical processing, and septic systems.

Pipe Volume Calculator

The pipe volume calculator uses the same cylinder formula but applies it to the inner diameter: V = pi x (inner radius)2 x length. Important: always use the inner diameter, not the outer diameter, for liquid volume calculations. This is essential for plumbing, irrigation design, and fire suppression system calculations.

Example — Irrigation pipe

Pipe with 4-inch inner diameter and 100 ft long: r = 2 in = 0.1667 ft, V = pi x 0.02778 x 100 = 8.73 ft3 = 65.3 gallons of water

Aquarium Volume Calculator

To find your aquarium volume, use the rectangular box formula for standard fish tanks: V = L x W x H. For a cylindrical aquarium, use the cylinder formula. A standard 40-gallon tank is approximately 36 x 18 x 16 inches. Knowing exact volume helps determine the correct filter flow rate, heater wattage, and safe fish stocking levels.

Pond Volume Calculator

Pond volume depends on the pond shape. A rectangular pond uses V = L x W x average depth. An oval or kidney-shaped pond can be approximated using the ellipsoid or cylinder formula with average dimensions. Pond volume is critical for calculating fish stocking density, water treatment dosages, and pump sizing for circulation and aeration.

Water and Liquid Volume Calculator

Any container volume can be converted to a water volume or liquid volume automatically using our conversion chips. Key conversions: 1 ft3 = 7.481 US gallons = 28.317 litres. 1 m3 = 1,000 litres = 264.2 US gallons. 1 US gallon = 3.785 litres = 231 in3.

Concrete Volume Calculator

Our concrete volume calculator uses the rectangular prism formula for slabs and the cylinder formula for round columns. Results are shown in cubic feet and automatically converted to cubic yards (divide ft3 by 27) since ready-mix concrete is sold by the cubic yard in the US.

Example — Driveway slab

Driveway 20 ft x 10 ft x 0.33 ft (4 inches) thick: V = 66 ft3 = 2.44 cubic yards. Order at least 2.7 yards to include 10% waste.

Medical Volume Calculators

Prostate Volume Calculator

The prostate volume calculator uses the prolate ellipsoid formula: V = 0.52 x length x width x height (all dimensions in centimeters, result in mL). Measurements are obtained from transrectal ultrasound (TRUS) or MRI. Normal prostate volume is 20–30 mL. Values above 30 mL may indicate benign prostatic hyperplasia (BPH).

Bladder Volume Calculator

The bladder volume calculator uses the ellipsoid formula: V = 0.52 x length x width x height (from ultrasound measurements in cm, result in mL). Normal bladder capacity is 300–500 mL. Post-void residual (PVR) volume above 100 mL may indicate urinary retention. Maximum cystometric capacity averages 400–600 mL in adults.

Tidal Volume Calculator

Tidal volume is the amount of air moved in or out of the lungs during each normal, relaxed breath. It is calculated using the formula: Tidal Volume = 6–8 mL x Ideal Body Weight (kg). Average adult tidal volume is approximately 500 mL (0.5 litres) at rest. This value is critical for setting mechanical ventilator parameters.

Common Questions

Volume Calculator FAQ

How do I calculate the volume of a cylinder?

Use the cylinder volume formula: V = pi x r2 x h where r is the radius (half the diameter) and h is the height or length. Example: a cylinder with diameter 6 ft and height 8 ft has r = 3 ft, so V = pi x 9 x 8 = 226.19 ft3 = 1,692 gallons. This same formula works for pipe volume, tank volume, and circular pool volume.

How do I calculate pool volume in gallons?

For a rectangular pool: Volume (ft3) = Length x Width x Average Depth, then multiply by 7.481 for US gallons. For a round pool: V = pi x r2 x depth x 7.481. Example: a 25 ft x 12 ft x 5 ft pool = 1,500 ft3 = 11,221 gallons. Select Pool in our Containers tab for an instant result with gallons and litres shown automatically.

How to calculate pipe volume?

Pipe volume = pi x (inner radius)2 x length. Always use the inner (bore) diameter, not the outside diameter. For a 3-inch inner diameter pipe, 50 feet long: r = 1.5 in = 0.125 ft, V = pi x 0.015625 x 50 = 2.45 ft3 = 18.34 gallons. Use the Pipe option in our Containers tab for step-by-step working.

How to calculate concrete volume in cubic yards?

Calculate volume in cubic feet first (L x W x D in feet), then divide by 27 to convert to cubic yards. Example: a driveway 20 ft x 10 ft x 4 inches thick (0.333 ft): V = 66.7 ft3 / 27 = 2.47 cubic yards. Always add 10% extra for waste and spillage, so order 2.7 yards. Our calculator converts to cubic yards automatically.

What is prostate volume and how is it calculated?

Prostate volume is calculated from ultrasound or MRI measurements using the prolate ellipsoid formula: V = 0.52 x length x width x height (in cm, result in mL). A normal prostate is 20–30 mL. Above 30 mL may indicate benign prostatic hyperplasia (BPH). Prostate volume also factors into PSA density calculations, which help distinguish BPH from prostate cancer. Always have results interpreted by a urologist.

What is tidal volume and what is the normal range?

Tidal volume is the volume of air moved per breath during normal, relaxed breathing. Normal adult tidal volume is approximately 500 mL (0.5 litres). It is estimated as 6–8 mL per kg of ideal body weight. For mechanically ventilated patients, the ARDSnet protocol recommends 6 mL/kg of predicted body weight to protect lung tissue. Tidal volume is measured during pulmonary function testing or spirometry.
