02 What is the formula for speed, distance, and time?
The three related formulas form the speed-distance-time triangle:
Speed = Distance ÷ Time
Distance = Speed × Time
Time = Distance ÷ Speed
Our calculator handles all three — just select which value you want to find, enter the other two known values, and get your result with all unit conversions shown at once.

06 How does the Distance Calculator work with coordinates?
Our Distance Calculator uses the Haversine formula to calculate the straight-line (as the crow flies) distance between two geographic coordinates. Enter the latitude and longitude of both points — or select from 8 common city pair presets. Results show in km, miles, meters, and nautical miles. The Haversine formula accounts for Earth's curvature for accurate geographic distances.
07 Can I calculate TV viewing distance and projector throw distance?
Yes. Our Distance Calculator includes a TV Viewing Distance mode (optimal seating distance for 4K, 1080p, and 8K by screen size) and a Projector Throw Distance mode (lens placement distance from screen using throw ratio). For a 65-inch 4K TV, the recommended distance is 5.4–8.1 feet. For a 100-inch screen with a 1.5:1 throw ratio, the projector goes 11.4 feet away.
08 What is the difference between straight-line distance and driving distance?
Straight-line distance (as the crow flies) is the shortest possible path between two points on Earth's surface — calculated using the Haversine formula. Driving distance is the actual road distance, which is always longer due to road routes, terrain, and detours — typically 1.2× to 1.5× the straight-line distance. Our Distance Calculator shows both: the exact straight-line distance plus estimated driving, walking, and flight distances.

12 What is the correct formula for average speed?
Average Speed = Total Distance ÷ Total Time. This is not the arithmetic mean of two speeds. Example: 60 km at 60 km/h (1 hour) then 60 km at 120 km/h (0.5 hours) → Average speed = 120 ÷ 1.5 = 80 km/h, not (60+120)÷2 = 90 km/h. Total time must include all stops and breaks. Our Average Speed Calculator handles multi-leg trips automatically.
13 What is the difference between average speed and instantaneous speed?
Average speed is the total distance divided by total time for an entire journey — it accounts for slowdowns, stops, and acceleration. Instantaneous speed is your speed at any single moment — what your speedometer shows right now. Our Speed Calculator computes average speed from total distance and time. For instantaneous speed, use your vehicle's tachometer with our RPM to Speed Calculator.

17 How does the RPM to Speed Calculator work?
Our RPM to Speed Calculator uses the formula: Speed (mph) = (RPM × π × Tire Diameter) ÷ (Gear Ratio × Axle Ratio × 1056). Enter your engine RPM (from your tachometer), tire size (diameter in inches or use our tire code converter for codes like 225/45R17), transmission gear ratio, and rear axle ratio. Results show in mph, km/h, and m/s, plus an all-gear speed chart for all 6 gears.
18 How do I enter a tire size code like 225/45R17?
In the RPM to Speed Calculator, select "Use tire size code" from the Tire Size dropdown. Then enter three values: Width (225), Aspect ratio (45), and Rim diameter (17). The calculator automatically computes the overall tire diameter using the formula: Diameter = (2 × Width × Aspect/100 + Rim × 25.4) ÷ 25.4. For 225/45R17 this gives 24.97 inches, which is used in the speed calculation.
19 Can the RPM Calculator work for bicycles and cycling cadence?
Yes. Select Bicycle/Motorcycle mode in our RPM to Speed Calculator. Enter your pedal cadence (RPM), select your wheel size (700c, 26", 27.5", etc.), and choose your gear ratio (chainring teeth ÷ sprocket teeth). Example: 90 RPM cadence, 50T/15T gear, 700c wheel → Wheel RPM = 90 × (50÷15) = 300 → Speed = 300 × π × 0.622 × 60 ÷ 1000 = 35.2 km/h.

Unit Conversions
3 Questions
20 How do I convert km/h to mph and vice versa?
km/h to mph: multiply by 0.62137. Example: 100 km/h × 0.62137 = 62.1 mph
mph to km/h: multiply by 1.60934. Example: 60 mph × 1.60934 = 96.6 km/h
Common conversions: 60 km/h = 37.3 mph · 80 km/h = 49.7 mph · 100 km/h = 62.1 mph · 120 km/h = 74.6 mph. Our Speed Calculator shows all 5 unit conversions simultaneously — no separate conversion step needed.
21 What is 1 knot in km/h and mph?
1 knot = 1.852 km/h = 1.151 mph = 0.514 m/s. A knot is one nautical mile per hour. It is the standard unit for maritime and aviation speeds. Common conversions: 10 knots = 18.5 km/h · 20 knots = 37.0 km/h · 30 knots (fast ship) = 55.6 km/h · 500 knots (commercial flight) = 926 km/h. Enter any value in knots in our Speed Calculator to see instant conversions to km/h, mph, and m/s.
22 What is Mach 1 in km/h and mph?
Mach 1 (speed of sound at sea level, 20°C) = 1,235 km/h = 767 mph = 343 m/s = 666 knots. The Mach number varies with altitude and temperature — at cruising altitude (~35,000 ft) Mach 1 ≈ 1,062 km/h. Common references: Mach 2 = 2,470 km/h · Commercial jets cruise at Mach 0.75–0.85 (830–940 km/h). Our speed calculator's unit reference table includes Mach number conversions.
