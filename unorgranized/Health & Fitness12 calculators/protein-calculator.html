Protein Calculator for Weight Loss and Muscle Gain

Our protein calculator for weight loss and muscle gain (body recomposition mode) uses the highest protein range (1.8–2.2g/kg) because building muscle while losing fat requires more protein than either goal alone. This is supported by a 2018 meta-analysis in the British Journal of Sports Medicine showing that protein intakes above 1.62g/kg maximize muscle gains even at a calorie deficit. For the protein calculator for weight loss female: women typically use 1.4–1.8g/kg. Both men and women benefit from spreading this across 3–4 meals of 25–40g protein each, triggering multiple bouts of muscle protein synthesis throughout the day.

Protein Calculator — Worked Examples

GLP-1 Protein Calculator — Ozempic, Wegovy, Zepbound, Mounjaro

Our GLP-1 protein calculator and Zepbound protein calculator are specifically designed for people taking GLP-1 receptor agonist medications. GLP-1 medications cause rapid weight loss — but without adequate protein and resistance training, studies show that 25–40% of weight lost comes from muscle mass rather than fat. Our calculator recommends 1.2–1.6g of protein per kg of current body weight per day for GLP-1 users. At low appetite levels common with these medications, prioritize protein-rich foods at every eating occasion: eggs, Greek yogurt, chicken, fish, cottage cheese, and protein shakes if needed to hit targets.

Protein Calculator for Seniors — Preventing Sarcopenia

Our protein calculator for seniors recommends 1.2–1.5g/kg per day — 50% more than the standard adult RDA of 0.8g/kg. This reflects extensive research showing that older adults (65+) have anabolic resistance — their muscles require more protein stimulus to achieve the same muscle protein synthesis response as younger adults. Sarcopenia (muscle loss with age) is a major cause of falls, fractures, and loss of independence. Even modestly increased protein intake (1.2g/kg vs 0.8g/kg) combined with resistance training significantly preserves muscle mass in older adults.

Menopause Protein Calculator

The menopause protein calculator recommends 1.2–1.6g of protein per kg of body weight for women going through or post-menopause. Estrogen decline during menopause accelerates muscle loss (up to 0.5% per year) and bone density reduction, making protein and resistance training more important than at any previous life stage. Higher protein intake during menopause: 1) Supports muscle mass retention. 2) Improves satiety during potential weight gain period. 3) Helps maintain bone strength. 4) May reduce hot flash severity. Combine protein intake with calcium (1,200mg/day) and vitamin D for comprehensive bone support.

CKD Protein Calculator — Kidney Disease Special Considerations

The CKD protein calculator is the only mode where lower protein is better (for non-dialysis patients). High protein intake creates metabolic waste products (urea, creatinine) that diseased kidneys struggle to filter, potentially accelerating kidney function decline. Stage 3–5 CKD (non-dialysis): 0.6–0.8g/kg/day. Stage 1–2 CKD: 0.8g/kg/day (standard RDA is acceptable). On dialysis: 1.1–1.5g/kg/day (more needed due to protein loss during dialysis sessions).

CKD protein warning: The CKD protein calculator results are estimates only. Incorrect protein intake can significantly accelerate kidney function decline. Always work with a nephrologist and renal registered dietitian to set your personal protein target. Phosphorus content of protein foods (high in dairy, nuts, beans) is equally important to manage in CKD.

Protein timing tip: Research shows that 25–40g of protein per meal maximizes muscle protein synthesis in adults. Consuming all your daily protein in one or two large meals leaves most of it unused for muscle building. Our protein calculator meal distribution table shows optimal per-meal targets. For athletes and muscle gain, a protein shake within 2 hours post-workout is beneficial — but the total daily amount matters more than timing for most people.

Common Questions

Protein Calculator FAQ

Questions covering protein for weight loss, muscle gain, GLP-1 medications, seniors, CKD, menopause, and food protein sources.

How much protein do I need per day?

Our how much protein calculator and daily protein calculator uses these evidence-based ranges: Sedentary adults (RDA): 0.8g per kg body weight. For 70kg person: 56g/day. Lightly active: 1.0g/kg = 70g/day. Moderately active: 1.2g/kg = 84g/day. Weight loss: 1.4–1.6g/kg = 98–112g/day for 70kg. Muscle building: 1.8–2.2g/kg = 126–154g/day for 70kg. Seniors 65+: 1.2–1.5g/kg. GLP-1 users: 1.2–1.6g/kg. CKD Stage 3–5: 0.6–0.8g/kg. Our online protein calculator applies the right formula for your specific goal and weight.

What protein calculator should I use for weight loss?

Our protein calculator for weight loss and protein calculator for weight loss female uses 1.2–1.6g per kg of goal body weight (not current weight). Higher protein during weight loss: 1) Preserves lean muscle mass during calorie deficit (muscle is metabolically active — losing it slows metabolism). 2) Increases satiety — protein is the most satiating macronutrient. 3) Has higher thermic effect — you burn 25–30% of protein calories just digesting it vs 5–8% for carbs. For women specifically: 1.4g/kg is a practical starting point. Our protein calculator for weight loss and muscle gain mode uses 1.8–2.2g/kg for those doing body recomposition (losing fat while gaining muscle simultaneously).

How much protein for muscle gain and muscle growth?

Our protein calculator for muscle gain, protein calculator for muscle growth, and protein calculator to build muscle uses 1.6–2.2g per kg of body weight based on the most comprehensive meta-analysis (Morton et al., 2018, British Journal of Sports Medicine): protein intakes above 1.62g/kg do not further increase muscle gain in most populations. However, during calorie deficits, going up to 2.2–2.6g/kg can help preserve existing muscle. Practical target: 2.0g/kg for most natural athletes training 3–5 days/week. Example: 80kg man doing strength training: 80 × 2.0 = 160g protein/day. Split across 4 meals: 40g per meal.

What is the GLP-1 protein calculator for Ozempic/Zepbound?

Our GLP-1 protein calculator and Zepbound protein calculator covers Ozempic, Wegovy, Mounjaro, Zepbound, Saxenda, and other GLP-1/GIP agonists. These medications suppress appetite significantly — making it easy to under-eat protein. Studies on GLP-1-induced weight loss show 25–40% of lost weight can be muscle without adequate protein + resistance training. Recommendation: 1.2–1.6g protein per kg of current body weight per day. Practical tips for GLP-1 users: prioritize protein at every meal before eating other foods; use Greek yogurt, eggs, cottage cheese, and protein shakes to hit targets despite reduced appetite; do resistance training 2–3×/week to signal muscle retention even in a large calorie deficit.

Tuna (canned): 30g/100g. Turkey breast: 29g/100g. Salmon: 25g/100g. Cottage cheese: 11g/100g. Greek yogurt: 10g/100g. Eggs: 13g/100g. Lentils (cooked): 9g/100g. Edamame: 11g/100g. Whey protein powder: 80g/100g. For the Chipotle protein calculator: Chicken (4oz serving) = 32g protein. Steak = 21g. Barbacoa = 24g. Tofu Sofritas = 8g. Black beans = 7g per serving. Is there a menopause protein calculator?

Yes — our menopause protein calculator recommends 1.2–1.6g of protein per kg of body weight per day for perimenopausal and postmenopausal women. This is higher than the standard female RDA (0.8g/kg) because estrogen decline during menopause significantly accelerates muscle loss (sarcopenia) and bone density loss. Research shows higher protein intake during menopause: preserves lean muscle mass, improves body composition (more fat loss relative to muscle), enhances bone health when combined with calcium and vitamin D, and may improve hot flash frequency in some women. Combine with resistance training 2–3 times per week for best results. Select "Menopause" mode in our protein calculator above.
