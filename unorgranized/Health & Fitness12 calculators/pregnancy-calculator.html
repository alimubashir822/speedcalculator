Pregnancy Calculator Due Date — How Is EDD Calculated?

The standard pregnancy calculator due date uses Naegele's Rule: EDD = LMP + 280 days. This assumes a 28-day cycle and ovulation on day 14. For irregular cycles, the LMP pregnancy calculator adjusts: if your cycle is 32 days, ovulation occurs on day 18 (not 14), so 4 extra days are added to the EDD. The due date pregnancy calculator formula is used by every OB/GYN worldwide. Only ~5% of babies are born exactly on the EDD — most arrive between weeks 38–42.

IVF Pregnancy Calculator — Transfer Date to Due Date

The IVF pregnancy calculator and pregnancy calculator IVF works differently because the embryo's exact age at transfer is known. For a Day 5 blastocyst transfer: EDD = Transfer Date + 261 days (because the embryo is already 5 days old, equivalent to 5 days past fertilization). For a Day 3 transfer: EDD = Transfer Date + 263 days. Our IVF pregnancy calculator also works for frozen embryo transfers (FET), egg donation, and gestational surrogacy — use the same formula based on embryo age at transfer.

Common Questions

Pregnancy Calculator FAQ

Questions covering due date calculation, IVF pregnancy calculator, dog pregnancy calculator, week-by-week tracking, weight gain, and how many weeks pregnant.

How is the pregnancy due date calculated?

The pregnancy calculator due date uses Naegele's Rule: EDD (Estimated Due Date) = First day of Last Menstrual Period (LMP) + 280 days (40 weeks). This assumes a regular 28-day cycle with ovulation on day 14. For a different cycle length, ovulation day adjusts: 24-day cycle = ovulation day 10, 32-day cycle = ovulation day 18. Our due date pregnancy calculator automatically adjusts for cycle length. An alternative formula: take LMP month, add 9 months, then add 7 days. Example: LMP March 15 → add 9 months = December 15 → add 7 days = December 22 EDD.

How does the IVF pregnancy calculator work?

Our IVF pregnancy calculator and pregnancy calculator IVF mode works as follows: For a Day 5 blastocyst transfer: EDD = Transfer Date + 261 days. For a Day 3 embryo transfer: EDD = Transfer Date + 263 days. The logic is that IVF embryos have a known age, so we subtract their age from the standard 266 days (38 weeks from fertilization): Day 5 blastocyst = 266 − 5 = 261. Day 3 cleavage = 266 − 3 = 263. This makes the pregnancy calculator IVF much more precise than LMP-based methods. Our calculator also handles frozen embryo transfers (FET) — use the transfer date, same formula applies.

How many weeks pregnant am I? How do I calculate it?

The pregnancy calculator how many weeks am I formula: Weeks pregnant = (Today − LMP) ÷ 7. Example: LMP was February 1, today is April 17 = 75 days apart ÷ 7 = 10 weeks + 5 days pregnant. Alternatively, use our "From Due Date" mode: enter your EDD and we calculate backward to tell you how many weeks pregnant you are today. Pregnancy is counted from LMP (not conception), so you are technically "2 weeks pregnant" at the moment of conception. Our pregnancy calculator week by week shows your exact week, trimester, and development stage.

What is the dog pregnancy calculator and how do I use it?

Our dog pregnancy calculator estimates the whelping (birth) date from the mating date. Dog gestation = 63 days from ovulation (not from mating), with a range of 58–68 days from first mating. If your dog was mated on one day, add 63 days for the expected whelping date, but be prepared from day 58. Our dog pregnancy calculator week by week pictures shows: Week 1 (implantation), Week 3 (heartbeats), Week 4 (ultrasound confirms pregnancy), Week 6 (fetuses fully formed), Week 9 (birth). Select "Dog Pregnancy" mode and enter the mating date — works for our cat pregnancy calculator and cow pregnancy calculator too.

How much weight should I gain during pregnancy?

Our weight gain pregnancy calculator and weight gain during pregnancy calculator uses ACOG guidelines: Normal BMI (18.5–24.9): gain 25–35 lbs (11–16 kg) total. Underweight (BMI under 18.5): 28–40 lbs. Overweight (BMI 25–29.9): 15–25 lbs. Obese (BMI 30+): 11–20 lbs. Twins: add ~10–15 lbs to all ranges. Timing: First trimester: 1–5 lbs total. Second and third trimesters: approximately 1 lb/week for normal BMI. Use the Weight Gain tab in our calculator to get week-by-week targets based on your pre-pregnancy BMI and current pregnancy week.

What is gender prediction in pregnancy and when can I know?

The due date pregnancy calculator gender prediction timeline: NIPT blood test (Non-Invasive Prenatal Testing): Week 10–13 — most accurate (99%+). Ultrasound gender determination: Week 18–20 (anatomy scan) — reliable 80–95%. Ramzi theory (based on placenta location): Week 6–8 — scientifically unproven. Nub theory (genital tubercle angle): Week 12–13 — about 70% accurate when done by expert. Chinese gender prediction calendar — no scientific basis.
