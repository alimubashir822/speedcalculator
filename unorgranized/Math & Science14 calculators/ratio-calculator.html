How to Simplify a Ratio — Step by Step

To simplify a ratio A:B, divide both numbers by their Greatest Common Divisor (GCD). The GCD is the largest number that divides both A and B without a remainder.

📘 Example — Simplify 24:36

Factors of 24: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 8, 12, 24
Factors of 36: 1, 2, 3, 4, 6, 9, 12, 18, 36
GCD(24, 36) = 12
24 ÷ 12 = 2  |  36 ÷ 12 = 3
Simplified ratio: 2:3

What Is My Debt to Income Ratio?

Your debt-to-income (DTI) ratio is the percentage of your gross monthly income (before taxes) that goes toward paying your monthly debts. It is one of the most important numbers lenders look at when you apply for a mortgage, car loan, or any other credit.

Formula: DTI = (Total Monthly Debt Payments ÷ Gross Monthly Income) × 100

How to Calculate Debt to Income Ratio

  1. Add up ALL monthly debt payments: mortgage/rent, car loans, student loans, credit card minimum payments, personal loans, child support, alimony
  2. Find your gross monthly income (your salary before tax, plus any other regular income)
  3. Divide total monthly debt by gross monthly income
  4. Multiply by 100 to get the percentage

📘 Example — How to Figure Out Debt to Income Ratio

Mortgage: $1,200/month
Car loan: $350/month
Student loan: $200/month
Credit cards: $150/month
Total debt: $1,900/month

Gross monthly income: $5,500
DTI = ($1,900 ÷ $5,500) × 100 = 34.5%
Assessment: Good — most lenders will approve

What is a good debt-to-income ratio? For mortgage approval, aim for a DTI below 36%. Below 20% is considered excellent. For FHA loans, the limit is typically 43%. For conventional loans, most lenders prefer 36% or less. Our DTI calculator shows your zone (Excellent / Good / Manageable / High Risk) automatically.

How to Solve a Proportion (Missing Value)

A proportion states that two ratios are equal: A:B = C:D. If any one value is missing, you can find it using cross-multiplication:

A × D = B × C

📘 Example — Find D: 3:4 = 9:?

Cross multiply: 3 × D = 4 × 9
3D = 36
D = 36 ÷ 3 = 12
Answer: 3:4 = 9:12 ✓ (both simplify to 3:4)

Aspect Ratio Calculator — Screen & Photo Sizes

The aspect ratio describes the proportional relationship between width and height. It is written as W:H. Common aspect ratios:

  • 16:9 — HD/Full HD/4K TV screens, YouTube, most phones (landscape)
  • 4:3 — Old TV screens, older monitors, some cameras
  • 1:1 — Instagram square posts, profile pictures
  • 2.39:1 — Cinematic / widescreen film format
  • 9:16 — Vertical/portrait phone video, TikTok, Reels

Rent to Income Ratio — What Is Affordable?

The common rule is that rent should not exceed 30% of your gross monthly income. This is calculated as: Rent Ratio = Monthly Rent ÷ Monthly Income × 100

Example: Rent of $1,500 / Income of $5,000 = 30% — right at the affordability limit. Use our Proportion mode to calculate this by entering: rent as A, income as B, 30 as C, and find D (the maximum rent you can afford at 30%).

Ratio Calculator FAQ

What is a debt-to-income ratio and how to calculate it?

The debt-to-income (DTI) ratio is the percentage of your gross monthly income that goes to debt payments. Formula: DTI = (Total Monthly Debt ÷ Gross Monthly Income) × 100. Include all monthly payments: mortgage, car, student loans, credit cards, personal loans. Use our DTI tab — enter each debt and your income, then click Calculate to get your DTI% and lender assessment instantly.

What is a good debt-to-income ratio for a mortgage?

For mortgage approval: Under 36% is ideal and qualifies for most conventional loans. 36-43% is acceptable — FHA loans allow up to 43%. 43-49% is risky and limits your options. 50% or above — most lenders will decline. The lower your DTI, the better your interest rate. Aim to reduce debts before applying if your DTI is above 36%.

How to simplify a ratio — what is the ratio calculator method?

Enter A and B into the Simplify tab and click Calculate. The calculator finds the Greatest Common Divisor (GCD) of both numbers, then divides both by it: Simplified = (A÷GCD) : (B÷GCD). For example, 18:12 → GCD=6 → 3:2. The result also shows the decimal form (18:12 = 1.5:1) and percentage split (60% : 40%).

How to find a missing value in a proportion?

Use our Proportion tab. Select which value (A, B, C, or D) is missing, enter the three known values, and click Calculate. The method is cross-multiplication: in A:B = C:D, if D is missing: D = (B × C) ÷ A. Example: 5:8 = 15:? → ? = (8 × 15) ÷ 5 = 24. Works for all four positions.

How to calculate aspect ratio for a new screen size?

Use the Aspect Ratio tab. Enter the original Width and Height (e.g. 1920 × 1080), then optionally enter a new Width to calculate the proportional Height. The calculator simplifies the ratio using GCD (1920:1080 → 16:9) and scales dimensions proportionally. This ensures images and videos are not distorted when resizing.

What is the rent-to-income ratio rule?

The standard rule is that rent should be no more than 30% of your gross monthly income. This is the rent-to-income ratio. Formula: Rent Ratio = (Monthly Rent ÷ Monthly Income) × 100. If you earn $4,000/month, maximum affordable rent is $1,200 (30%). Use our DTI calculator — enter rent as a debt payment — to see how rent affects your overall DTI ratio.
