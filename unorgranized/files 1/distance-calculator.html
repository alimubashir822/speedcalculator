Driving Distance Calculator — Road vs. Straight-Line

The driving distance calculator and driving mileage calculator estimate actual road distance by multiplying straight-line distance by a 1.3 route multiplier. This is because roads must follow terrain, existing infrastructure, and legal routes. A straight-line distance of 100 km becomes approximately 130 km by road. For distance conversion calculator needs — converting km to miles, meters to feet, or nautical miles — our results panel shows all units simultaneously. TV Viewing Distance Calculator

The TV viewing distance calculator and TV distance calculator determines optimal seating distance based on screen size and resolution. Formula: For 4K: Distance = Screen Size (inches) × 1.0 to 1.5. For 1080p: Distance = Screen Size × 1.5 to 2.5. The Society of Motion Picture and Television Engineers (SMPTE) recommends a 30-degree viewing angle, which translates to approximately 1.6× the screen width. A 65-inch 4K TV has a recommended viewing distance of 5.4–8.1 feet (1.65–2.47 meters).

Projector Throw Distance Calculator

The projector throw distance calculator and projector distance calculator formula: Throw Distance = Screen Width × Throw Ratio. Screen Width = Screen Diagonal × 0.872 (16:9 aspect). For a 120-inch screen: Width = 120 × 0.872 = 104.6 inches. With 1.5:1 throw ratio: Throw distance = 104.6 × 1.5 = 156.9 inches = 13.1 feet (3.99 m). Ultra short throw projectors (0.4–0.6:1) can sit 2–4 feet from the screen. Short throw (0.7–0.9:1) at 4–6 feet. Standard projectors (1.2–2.0:1) typically 8–20 feet depending on screen size.

Pupillary Distance Calculator

The pupillary distance calculator and vertex distance calculator measures the distance between the centers of your pupils in millimeters. Binocular PD = Right Monocular PD + Left Monocular PD. Average adult binocular PD: men 64mm, women 62mm. Range: 54–74mm. To measure at home: hold a ruler against your forehead, close your right eye, align 0mm with your left pupil center, then open and close eyes to measure to the right pupil center. Monocular PD is needed for progressive lenses and high-prescription glasses. Our pupillary distance calculator shows your binocular PD and recommended near PD (binocular PD minus 3mm).

Golf Club Distance Calculator

The golf club distance calculator estimates carry distance for each club based on swing speed. Driver distance ≈ Swing Speed (mph) × 2.3–2.5. At 90 mph: Driver ≈ 207–225 yards, 5-iron ≈ 154–168 yards, 7-iron ≈ 131–143 yards, 9-iron ≈ 108–118 yards, Pitching Wedge ≈ 86–94 yards. Increasing swing speed by 10 mph adds approximately 23–28 yards to driver distance. Distance Calculator FAQ

Answers about calculate distance online, Haversine formula, driving vs. straight-line distance, TV viewing, projector throw, and more.

How do I calculate the distance between two points using coordinates?

To calculate distance online between two geographic points, use our latitude longitude distance calculator — enter the latitude and longitude of both locations in decimal degrees (e.g., New York: 40.7128°N, -74.0060°W). The Haversine formula accounts for Earth's curvature: d = 2R × arcsin(√(sin²(Δlat/2) + cos(lat1)×cos(lat2)×sin²(Δlon/2))). This gives the exact straight line distance calculator result — the shortest path along Earth's surface, also called "as the crow flies." Our coordinate distance calculator shows the result in km, miles, meters, and nautical miles.

What is the difference between the distance calculator and Google Maps distance?

Our distance calculator calculates straight-line distance (crow fly distance) using the Haversine formula on coordinates — this is the true shortest distance between two points on Earth. The google map distance calculator (Google Maps) shows actual road/walking distances following real routes, which is always longer. For a driving distance calculator result, our tool multiplies the straight-line distance by 1.3 as an estimate. For exact calculate driving distance between two locations via real roads, the distance calculator google maps is the best tool. Our calculator complements Google Maps by providing the geographic baseline distance.

How does the TV viewing distance calculator work?

The TV viewing distance calculator and TV distance calculator is based on screen resolution and human visual acuity. For 4K Ultra HD: optimal viewing distance = screen size (inches) × 1.0 to 1.5. For 1080p Full HD: screen size × 1.5 to 2.5. For 8K: screen size × 0.75 to 1.0. Sitting closer than minimum wastes resolution detail your eyes can't distinguish at 1080p. Sitting farther than maximum loses immersion. Example: 65-inch 4K TV → minimum 3.5 feet, recommended 5.4–8.1 feet, maximum 12 feet.

How do I calculate projector throw distance?

The projector throw distance calculator and projector distance calculator formula: Throw Distance = Screen Width × Throw Ratio. For a 16:9 screen: Screen Width = Screen Diagonal × 0.872. Example: 120-inch screen → Width = 120 × 0.872 = 104.6 inches. Standard projector (throw ratio 1.5:1): 104.6 × 1.5 = 156.9 inches = 13.1 feet (3.99 m). Ultra short throw (0.5:1): 104.6 × 0.5 = 52.3 inches = 4.4 feet. Use the Projector mode in our projector throw distance calculator above to get your exact placement distance.

What is pupillary distance and how do I measure it?

Pupillary distance (PD) measured by our pupillary distance calculator is the distance between your pupil centers in millimeters — needed for lens centering in eyeglasses. To measure: stand 8 inches from a mirror, hold a ruler against your forehead, close your right eye and align 0mm with your left pupil. Open your right eye, close your left eye, and read the measurement at your right pupil. This is your binocular PD. Average adult: 54–74mm (men ~64mm, women ~62mm). Our vertex distance calculator also shows the near PD (binocular PD − 3mm) used for reading glasses.

How does the golf club distance calculator work?

The golf club distance calculator estimates carry distance (yards) for each club using swing speed as the primary input. Driver formula: Carry ≈ Swing Speed (mph) × 2.3 to 2.5. Irons are calculated as percentages of driver distance: 3-iron ≈ 80%, 5-iron ≈ 70%, 7-iron ≈ 60%, 9-iron ≈ 50%, PW ≈ 42%. At 90 mph swing speed: Driver ≈ 216 yards, 5-iron ≈ 151 yards, 7-iron ≈ 130 yards, 9-iron ≈ 108 yards, PW ≈ 91 yards. These are carry distances — add 5–15 yards for roll on normal conditions.
