FAQ

Travel Time Calculator FAQ

Common questions about calculate travel time, driving hours, arrival time, and road trip planning.

How do I calculate travel time from distance and speed?

The travel time formula is: Time = Distance ÷ Speed. For a 300 km journey at 100 km/h: 300 ÷ 100 = 3 hours. For 250 miles at 60 mph: 250 ÷ 60 = 4.167 hours = 4 hours 10 minutes. To convert decimal hours to minutes: multiply the decimal part by 60. Our travel time calculator does this automatically — just enter your distance and average speed, and it shows the exact travel duration plus estimated arrival time.

How accurate is the driving time calculator?

Our driving time calculator is mathematically exact for the average speed you enter. Real accuracy depends on how realistic your speed estimate is. For highway trips, use 85–100 km/h (53–62 mph) average. For mixed routes, use 60–75 km/h. Add 10–20% buffer for traffic lights, construction, and slowdowns. Use the Stop Time field to add fuel breaks and rest stops for a more realistic road trip calculator estimate.

How do I calculate my arrival time?

The arrival time calculator formula: Arrival = Departure Time + Travel Duration + Stop Time. Example: depart 8:30 AM, driving time 3h 45m, 30-minute stops = arrive at 12:45 PM. In our trip time calculator, toggle "Arrival Time Calculator," enter your departure date and time, and it automatically calculates your ETA. Overnight journeys that cross midnight are handled correctly, showing the next day's date.

How long does it take to drive 100 miles or 100 km?

Driving 100 km takes: 50 minutes at 120 km/h, 1 hour at 100 km/h, 1 hour 15 minutes at 80 km/h, 1 hour 40 minutes at 60 km/h. Driving 100 miles takes: 1 hour 20 minutes at 75 mph, 1 hour 40 minutes at 60 mph, 2 hours at 50 mph. Use our driving time calculator above for any exact distance and speed combination.

What average speed should I use for a road trip calculator?

For road trip calculator accuracy, use realistic averages: USA/Canada highway: 65–75 mph (105–120 km/h), Europe motorway: 80–90 mph (130–145 km/h), Mixed highway: 55–65 mph (88–105 km/h), City: 25–35 mph (40–55 km/h). For a multi-day road trip, 55 mph (88 km/h) average is a safe estimate that accounts for stops and slowdowns. Our travel time estimator uses these defaults when you select a transport mode.

Can I use this as a commute time calculator?

Yes — our commute time calculator mode works by entering your daily commute distance and selecting your transport mode. For a 20 km car commute at 50 km/h average: 20 ÷ 50 = 0.4 hours = 24 minutes. For the same 20 km by bicycle at 20 km/h: 20 ÷ 20 = 1 hour. Toggle the Arrival Time Calculator and enter your required arrival time to work backwards to your needed departure time.
