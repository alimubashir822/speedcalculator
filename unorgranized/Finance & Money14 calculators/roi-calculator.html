ROI Formula — Simple, Annualized, and Net Profit

The basic simple ROI calculator formula: ROI (%) = (Net Profit ÷ Investment Cost) × 100. Net Profit = Total Return − Investment Cost. For comparing investments of different durations, annualized ROI is more accurate: Annualized ROI = ((1 + ROI/100)^(1/Years) − 1) × 100.

Rental Property ROI Calculator

The rental property ROI calculator uses two key metrics: Cash-on-Cash Return and Cap Rate. Cash-on-Cash = Annual Net Income ÷ Total Cash Invested. Cap Rate = Net Operating Income ÷ Property Value. The rental ROI calculator is most useful when you include all actual expenses: mortgage, property taxes, insurance, maintenance (budget 1–2% of property value/year), property management (8–10% of rent), and vacancy allowance (5–8%).

ROI Calculator FAQ

Questions for simple ROI, rental property ROI, marketing ROI, real estate ROI, solar ROI, Bitcoin, Amazon, SEO, college, and CRM ROI calculators.

What is ROI and how is it calculated?

ROI (Return on Investment) measures how much profit you made relative to what you invested. Formula: ROI (%) = (Net Profit ÷ Investment Cost) × 100. Net Profit = Total Return − Investment Cost. Example: invest $10,000, receive $13,500 back → Net Profit = $3,500 → ROI = ($3,500 ÷ $10,000) × 100 = 35%. For comparing investments of different durations, use annualized ROI: ((1 + ROI/100)^(1/Years) − 1) × 100. Our simple ROI calculator computes both instantly.

What is a good ROI for rental property?

A good rental property ROI depends on your market and goals. Cash-on-cash return benchmarks: 6–8% = good for appreciating markets like major cities; 8–12% = strong return for cash flow-focused investors; 12%+ = excellent (usually in lower-cost markets or high-density rentals. Cap rates follow similar ranges: 4–5% (primary markets like NYC/SF), 6–8% (secondary markets), 8%+ (tertiary markets). Our rental property ROI calculator computes both cash-on-cash return and cap rate simultaneously.

How do I calculate SEO ROI and marketing ROI?

SEO ROI formula: ROI = ((Revenue from SEO − SEO Cost) ÷ SEO Cost) × 100. To calculate revenue from SEO: Sessions × Conversion Rate × Average Order Value. Example: SEO investment $2,000/month, generates 500 monthly leads at $4,000 value → monthly revenue = $4,000. Annual ROI = (($48,000 − $24,000) ÷ $24,000) × 100 = 100% ROI. Enterprise SEO ROI often reaches 400–800% as organic traffic compounds over 12–24 months. Use our marketing ROI calculator mode with total SEO spend and attributed revenue for your full ROI analysis.

How does the solar ROI calculator work?

Our solar ROI calculator computes: Net System Cost = Gross Cost − Federal Tax Credit (30% in 2024) − State Incentives. Annual Savings = Annual kWh production × Local electricity rate. Payback Period = Net Cost ÷ Annual Savings. Total ROI over 25 years = ((Lifetime Savings − Net Cost) ÷ Net Cost) × 100. Example: $22,000 system, $6,600 tax credit (30%), $1,900/year savings: Net cost = $15,400. Payback = 8.1 years. 25-year ROI = ($47,500 − $15,400) ÷ $15,400 × 100 = 208% ROI. Use the Solar mode in our calculator above.

What is annualized ROI and why is it important?

Annualized ROI converts any total return into a yearly equivalent so you can fairly compare investments of different durations. Without it, a 50% ROI looks the same whether earned in 1 year or 10 years — but they're very different performance levels. Formula: Annualized ROI = ((1 + Total ROI/100)^(1/Years) − 1) × 100. Example: 50% total ROI over 3 years → Annualized = (1.50^(1/3) − 1) × 100 = 14.5%/year. 50% ROI in 1 year = 50% annualized. Our ROI calculator shows both total and annualized ROI automatically for every calculation.
