Stock Profit Calculator — How It Works

The stock profit calculator computes: Net Profit = (Sell Price − Buy Price) × Shares − Brokerage Fees. ROI = (Net Profit ÷ Total Investment) × 100. Profit Margin = (Net Profit ÷ Total Revenue) × 100.

Stock Profit Calculator Example

Buy: 50 shares at $40 = $2,000 total cost
Sell: 50 shares at $58 = $2,900 total revenue
Brokerage fee: $10 each way = $20 total
Net Profit = $2,900 − $2,000 − $20 = $880
ROI = ($880 ÷ $2,000) × 100 = 44%
Profit Margin = ($880 ÷ $2,900) × 100 = 30.3%

Profit Calculator FAQ

Answers for stock profit, crypto profit, eBay, Etsy, Amazon, gross profit, options, home sale, and ROI questions.

How do I calculate profit from buying and selling stocks?

Use the stock profit calculator formula: Net Profit = (Sell Price − Buy Price) × Shares − Fees. Profit % = (Net Profit ÷ Total Cost) × 100. Example: buy 100 shares at $45, sell at $62. Gross profit = ($62 − $45) × 100 = $1,700. After $20 fees: Net profit = $1,680. Profit % = $1,680 ÷ $4,500 × 100 = 37.3% ROI. Switch to Investment → Stocks mode in our calculator above and enter your buy/sell price, quantity, and fees for an instant result.

How does the crypto profit calculator work for Bitcoin and Dogecoin?

Our crypto profit calculator works for Bitcoin (BTC), Dogecoin, XRP, Ethereum, and any cryptocurrency. Formula: Profit = (Exit Price − Entry Price) × Quantity − Exchange Fees. Bitcoin profit calculator example: buy 0.25 BTC at $35,000 = $8,750 invested. Sell at $60,000 = $15,000 proceeds. Exchange fees 1% = $237.50. Net profit = $15,000 − $8,750 − $237.50 = $6,012.50 (68.7% ROI). Works identically as a Dogecoin profit calculator, XRP profit calculator, or any coin.

What is the difference between gross profit and net profit?

Gross profit = Revenue − Cost of Goods Sold (COGS) only. It ignores operating expenses, platform fees, and other costs. Net profit = Revenue − All costs including fees, shipping, advertising, and overhead. Example on eBay: sell for $100, cost $60. Gross profit = $40 (40% margin). After eBay fees of $15 and shipping $8: Net profit = $17 (17% net margin). Our gross profit calculator and net profit calculator show both simultaneously. For accurate decision-making, always use net profit.

How do I calculate home sale profit with the house profit calculator?

Our home sale profit calculator formula: Net Profit = Sale Price − Purchase Price − Realtor Commission − Closing Costs − Improvements − Capital Gains Tax. Real example: Bought for $320,000, sold for $500,000. Realtor 6% = $30,000. Closing costs 1.5% = $7,500. Improvements = $15,000. Net profit = $500,000 − $320,000 − $30,000 − $7,500 − $15,000 = $127,500. ROI = $127,500 ÷ $320,000 = 39.8%. Use the Real Estate mode in our house profit calculator above for any scenario.

How much do eBay and Etsy fees reduce my profit?

eBay fees typically total 12.5–15% of sale price (12.9% final value fee + 2.7% payment processing + $0.30). On a $100 sale: ~$15 in fees. Etsy fees total ~10%: 6.5% transaction fee + 3% payment processing + $0.25 + $0.20 listing. On a $100 Etsy sale: ~$10 in fees. Our eBay profit calculator and Etsy profit calculator auto-apply these rates — just enter your item cost and sale price to see your true net profit and whether your pricing is profitable.

What is ROI and how is it different from profit margin?

ROI (Return on Investment) = (Net Profit ÷ Total Cost) × 100 — measures return relative to what you spent. Profit Margin = (Net Profit ÷ Revenue) × 100 — measures what % of revenue is profit. Example: cost $60, sell $100, net profit $40. ROI = $40 ÷ $60 × 100 = 66.7% (66.7 cents profit per dollar spent). Profit Margin = $40 ÷ $100 × 100 = 40% (40 cents profit per dollar earned). ROI is always higher than margin for the same transaction. Our profit calculator shows both metrics automatically.

How does the options profit calculator work?

The options profit calculator and futures profit calculator calculate: Profit = (Exit Price − Entry Price) × Quantity × Contract Multiplier − Fees. For a standard equity option: 1 contract = 100 shares. If you buy a call option at $3.00 premium and sell at $7.50: Profit = ($7.50 − $3.00) × 1 × 100 = $450 per contract. ROI = $450 ÷ $300 = 150%. Select Investment → Options in our calculator, enter entry premium, exit premium, and number of contracts for instant results including break-even strike price.
