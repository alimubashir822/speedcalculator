How to Add Fractions — Step by Step

Adding fractions requires a common denominator. Follow these steps:

  1. Find the Least Common Multiple (LCM) of both denominators
  2. Convert each fraction to an equivalent fraction with the LCM as the denominator
  3. Add the numerators while keeping the denominator the same
  4. Simplify the result using the Greatest Common Divisor (GCD)

📘 Example — Adding Fractions: 1/2 + 1/3

Step 1: LCM of 2 and 3 = 6
Step 2: 1/2 = 3/6 and 1/3 = 2/6
Step 3: 3/6 + 2/6 = 5/6
Step 4: GCD of 5 and 6 = 1 (already simplified)
Answer: 5/6 = 0.8333...

How to Simplify a Fraction (Reduce Fraction Calculator)

To simplify a fraction means reducing it to its lowest terms. Use the reduce fraction calculator mode for this. The process uses the Greatest Common Divisor (GCD):

  1. Find the GCD of the numerator and denominator
  2. Divide both numerator and denominator by the GCD
  3. The result is the simplified fraction

📘 Example — Simplify 36/48

GCD of 36 and 48 = 12
36 ÷ 12 = 3 and 48 ÷ 12 = 4
Simplified: 3/4 = 0.75

How to Convert Decimal to Fraction

For Terminating Decimals (e.g. 0.75)

Use our decimal to fraction calculator mode. The process:

  1. Count the decimal places (0.75 has 2 places)
  2. Write as a fraction over the corresponding power of 10: 75/100
  3. Simplify: GCD of 75 and 100 = 25, so 75/100 = 3/4

For Repeating Decimals (e.g. 0.333...)

Tick the "Repeating decimal" checkbox in our repeating decimal to fraction calculator mode:

  1. Let x = 0.333...
  2. Multiply both sides by 10: 10x = 3.333...
  3. Subtract: 10x − x = 3.333... − 0.333... → 9x = 3
  4. Solve: x = 3/9 = 1/3

How to Use Mixed Number Fractions

A mixed number (or mixed fraction) has a whole number part and a fraction part — like 2¾ (two and three-quarters). To operate on mixed numbers:

  1. Convert each mixed number to an improper fraction: 2¾ = (2×4+3)/4 = 11/4
  2. Perform the operation (add, subtract, multiply, divide)
  3. Convert the result back to a mixed number if needed

📘 Example — Mixed Fraction Calculator: 2¾ + 1½

2¾ = 11/4 and 1½ = 3/2
LCM of 4 and 2 = 4
11/4 + 6/4 = 17/4
Answer: 17/4 = 4¼ = 4.25

Pro Tip: When dividing fractions, flip (reciprocal) the second fraction and multiply. This is called "Keep, Change, Flip" — Keep the first fraction, Change ÷ to ×, and Flip the second fraction. Example: 3/4 ÷ 2/5 = 3/4 × 5/2 = 15/8.

Improper Fractions Explained

An improper fraction has a numerator larger than or equal to the denominator (e.g. 7/4, 9/3). Our improper fraction calculator automatically converts results to mixed numbers when appropriate, so you always see both forms. For example, 7/4 = 1¾ and 9/3 = 3 (a whole number).

Fraction Comparison — Which Fraction Is Bigger?

To compare fractions quickly, convert both to decimals by dividing numerator by denominator, then compare. For example:

  • 3/4 = 0.75
  • 4/5 = 0.80
  • Since 0.80 > 0.75, 4/5 is bigger than 3/4

Use our Compare mode to get the answer instantly along with the decimal values for both fractions.

In our online fraction calculator, enter the numerator (top number) in the upper input box and the denominator (bottom number) in the lower box. The blue horizontal line between them visually represents the fraction bar — just like writing it on paper.

How to convert a fraction to a decimal without a calculator?

Simply divide the numerator by the denominator using long division. For example: 3/4 = 3 ÷ 4 = 0.75. For repeating decimals like 1/3 = 1 ÷ 3 = 0.333..., the division never ends. Use our Fraction → Decimal mode to convert instantly with full decimal precision.

Which fraction is bigger?

To compare fractions, convert them to decimals (divide numerator by denominator) or find a common denominator and compare numerators. For example: 3/4 = 0.75 vs 2/3 = 0.6667, so 3/4 is bigger. Use our Compare Fractions mode for an instant answer with full explanation.
