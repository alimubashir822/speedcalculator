Understanding Gear Ratios & Speed

Our car speed calculator gear ratio tool shows how each gear ratio affects vehicle speed. Understanding gear ratios is essential for performance tuning, fuel economy, and finding the right axle ratio for your driving style.

Car speed calculator gear ratio result — 6,000 RPM, 3.73 axle, 26.8" tire: 1st gear 28 mph · 2nd 40 mph · 3rd 58 mph · 4th 83 mph · 5th 99 mph · 6th OD 134 mph. Use the calculator above for your vehicle.
2.73:1
Economy Axle

Best for highway fuel economy. Lower RPM at highway speed. Less acceleration punch but excellent cruising efficiency. Common in highway trucks and economy sedans.

3.73:1
Performance Balance

Excellent balance of acceleration and highway ability. The most popular axle ratio for performance vehicles and towing trucks. Our car speed calculator default.

4.10:1
Sport/Towing

Strong off-the-line acceleration and towing torque. Higher RPM at highway speed uses more fuel. Popular in muscle cars and heavy-duty trucks. Reduces top speed slightly.

4.56:1
Drag Racing

Maximum launch acceleration for drag racing. Very high engine RPM at highway speeds — impractical for daily driving. Our race car speed calculator mode handles this.

RC Car Speed Calculator — Pinion, Spur & Motor RPM

The RC car speed calculator works differently from a standard vehicle calculator because RC cars use electric motors with very high RPM ratings (15,000–50,000+ RPM) and simpler gear reduction ratios. The formula: Speed (mph) = (Motor RPM × Wheel Diameter × π) ÷ (Gear Ratio × 63,360). Gear ratio = Spur teeth ÷ Pinion teeth. Example: 30,000 RPM motor, 20-tooth pinion, 80-tooth spur (4:1 ratio), 4-inch wheel: Speed = (30,000 × 4 × 3.14159) ÷ (4 × 63,360) = 56 mph. To increase RC car top speed: use a larger pinion gear (less reduction, more speed) or a smaller spur gear. To increase acceleration: smaller pinion or larger spur. Common Questions

Car Speed Calculator FAQ

Questions covering gear ratio speed calculation, RC car speed, tire size effects, axle ratio selection, and the speed formula.

How do you calculate car speed from gear ratio and RPM?

Our car speed calculator gear ratio uses this formula: Speed (MPH) = (RPM × Tire Diameter × π) ÷ (Transmission Gear Ratio × Axle Ratio × 336). The 336 constant equals 336 = 60 min/hr × 12 in/ft × 2 (to get radius not diameter) — or more precisely, it's the unit conversion factor to get MPH from inches and RPM. For car speed calculator km/h: multiply MPH result by 1.60934. Example: 6,000 RPM, 4th gear (1.00:1), 3.73 axle, 26.8-inch tire = (6,000 × 26.8 × 3.14159) ÷ (1.00 × 3.73 × 336) = 505,019 ÷ 1,253 = 83 MPH = 134 km/h. Our car speed calculator online does all this automatically for all 6 gears.

How does tire size affect car speed?

Larger tires increase speed at the same RPM because each tire rotation covers more ground. A 1-inch increase in tire diameter increases speed by approximately 3.5–4%. Example: changing from a 27-inch tire to a 31-inch tire on the same axle ratio and RPM increases speed by 14.8%. This is why lifted trucks with larger tires need recalibrated speedometers — the speedometer still reads the old tire size. Our car speed calculator mph shows the exact speed change when you update tire diameter. Use the P-metric converter in our calculator to convert your tire size (e.g., 265/70R17 = 31.6 inches) to diameter.

What is the RC car speed calculator formula?

Our RC car speed calculator formula: Speed (MPH) = (Motor KV × Battery Voltage × Wheel Diameter × π) ÷ (Pinion/Spur Ratio × 63,360). Or simply: (Motor RPM × Wheel Diameter × π) ÷ (Gear Ratio × 63,360) where Gear Ratio = Spur ÷ Pinion. The 63,360 = 12 inches/foot × 5,280 feet/mile. For a 3,000 KV motor on 3S LiPo (11.1V): Motor RPM = 3,000 × 11.1 = 33,300 RPM. With 20-tooth pinion, 76-tooth spur (3.8:1 ratio), 4-inch wheel: Speed = (33,300 × 4 × 3.14159) ÷ (3.8 × 63,360) = 418,407 ÷ 240,768 = 57 MPH. Select RC Car mode in our calculator and enter these values.

How do I choose the right axle ratio for my car?

Use our car speed calculator online Speed → RPM reverse mode to find your ideal axle ratio: 1) Enter your target highway cruising speed (e.g., 65 mph). 2) Enter your ideal cruise RPM (e.g., 1,800–2,200 for most engines). 3) Enter your tire size. 4) Adjust axle ratio until the calculated RPM matches your target. Lower ratio (2.73:1) = lower RPM = better fuel economy. Higher ratio (4.10:1) = higher RPM = more torque multiplication = better acceleration but worse economy. For towing: 3.73–4.10. For fuel economy: 2.73–3.08. For performance balance: 3.55–3.73. For drag racing: 4.10–4.56+. Our race car speed calculator and standard modes both support this analysis.
